The First Step in a New Land

Seattle • March 1998

I arrived in Seattle on a rainy day. I was 18 years old, with one suitcase and $200 in my pocket. Everything was new, everything was big, and I was both excited and scared...

The smell of the ocean hit me first as I stepped off the bus. It was different from Guangzhou - colder, sharper. The city stretched out before me, grey buildings reaching toward grey skies, and I remember thinking: this is it. This is where my new life begins.

I didn't know anyone. I had an address written on a crumpled piece of paper - a hostel someone had recommended back home. As I walked through the streets, pulling my suitcase behind me, I felt so small. The buildings were tall, the people moved quickly, and everyone spoke English that I could barely understand...
